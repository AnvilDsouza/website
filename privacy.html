Privacy Policy

Last updated: October 2025

This website (anvildsouza.github.io) is operated by Anvil D’Souza. I respect your privacy and am committed to protecting your personal data. This policy explains how I handle information collected through this website.

1. Information I Collect

This site is designed for professional engagement and does not collect personal information unless you voluntarily provide it (for example, by subscribing to a newsletter, contacting me, or interacting with linked platforms).

  • Usage data: Anonymous metrics such as page visits, time on site, and link clicks (via Plausible Analytics).
  • Contact information: Only if you submit it directly (e.g., email or form submission).
  • No cookies: This website does not use tracking cookies or cross-site trackers.

2. How I Use Your Data

The data collected is used solely to:

  • Respond to professional inquiries or collaborations

3. Data Storage & Security

This site does not store personal data on its own servers. Any analytics data is processed in aggregate form via Plausible Analytics, a GDPR-compliant, privacy-first platform hosted in the EU. If you contact me directly, your email will be stored only within my secure professional inbox.

4. Third-Party Services

External links (e.g., YouTube, Medium, LinkedIn, Substack) may have their own privacy policies. I have no control over and assume no responsibility for their content, privacy policies, or practices.

5. Ethical Analytics & AI Transparency

In alignment with my professional work on Responsible AI and Governance, this website follows the same principles of transparency, minimal data collection, and user trust.

  • No cookies, fingerprinting, or personal profiling are used.
  • All analytics data is aggregated, anonymized, and open to audit via Plausible.io.
  • The intent is educational: to understand engagement, not to exploit attention.
  • In the future, if AI models are integrated on this site, they will be governed under ethical transparency guidelines consistent with SAiGE principles.
“Ethical technology begins where analytics ends; when data serves humans, not the other way around.”
